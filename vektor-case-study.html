Vektor

A B2B SaaS tool that streamlines the UX research synthesis process, bringing a structured approach to qualitative data analysis for product teams.

My Role

  • UX Researcher
  • Product Designer
  • UI/UX Designer

Project Type

Personal Project (B2B SaaS)

Timeline

4 weeks (Summer 2023)

The Project

Vektor is a personal project I created to solve a common problem I've faced as a UX Researcher and Designer: the messy, manual process of synthesizing qualitative research data. The goal of this project was to design and prototype a tool that empowers product teams to quickly analyze user interviews, surveys, and other qualitative data, and to turn those findings into actionable product insights.

The Challenge

How might we give product teams a structured, efficient way to identify patterns and themes in large amounts of qualitative data, making research synthesis a collaborative and scalable process?

The Process

Academic & Market Research

I began by exploring existing tools for research synthesis and academic frameworks like grounded theory. I identified a gap in the market for a tool that could effectively balance a structured approach with the flexibility needed for qualitative data.

Early-Stage Ideation & Collaborative Sketching

Working with a product manager, I ran a collaborative sketching session to brainstorm initial workflows and feature sets. This early ideation was key to building a shared understanding and prioritizing core functionalities.

Agile & Deadline-Driven Prototyping

Over a single weekend, I designed and built a high-fidelity prototype in Figma. I adopted an agile approach, prioritizing core user flows and key interactions to have something testable in a short timeframe.

Iterative & Collaborative Feedback

I conducted usability testing with product managers and UX researchers, gathering feedback on the prototype. This allowed for rapid iteration, refining the product's interface and core functionality based on real user interactions.

The Outcome

The final prototype successfully demonstrated a structured workflow for research synthesis, providing a clear path from raw data to actionable insights. It validated the need for a tool that makes qualitative analysis a more efficient and collaborative process for product teams.

Moving Forward

Future iterations would focus on building out key features like customizable tagging, automated pattern detection, and a team dashboard for real-time collaboration. The next steps would involve full-stack development, transitioning from a prototype to a fully functional MVP.

Conclusion & Key Learnings

Vektor was a great exercise in applying a full UX process to a problem I had experienced myself. It taught me the value of a lean, agile approach to prototyping and reinforced the importance of collaborative design. This project showcases my ability to not only identify a problem but to also design and build a potential solution from start to finish.
