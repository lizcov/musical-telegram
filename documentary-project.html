Media vs. Reality

A master's thesis documentary exploring the lived experiences of vehicle dwellers and challenging common perceptions.

My Role

  • Researcher
  • Strategist & Producer
  • Storyteller

Project Type

Master's Thesis Documentary

Date

2021

The Project

This project served as my Master of Science thesis at the Georgia Institute of Technology. I chose to address vehicle dwelling and its perception as a lifestyle in the Americas, and the vulnerabilities different populations encounter as vehicle dwellers. The chosen medium was a documentary, which allowed for a compelling and empathetic narrative.

The Process

My approach was rooted in deep, empathetic research. The process involved:

Research & Problem Discovery

I began by defining the problem space and conducting in-depth academic research. I identified the discrepancy between the romanticized view of "van life" and the complex realities faced by vehicle dwellers.

Empathy Through Interviews

I gathered insights through interviews, demonstrating adaptability by pivoting to local and virtual interviews during the pandemic. I then synthesized these insights into a cohesive and compelling narrative.

Conclusion & Key Learnings

This project was a testament to my ability to conduct deep, empathetic research, identify and articulate a complex problem, and communicate findings in a compelling way. It demonstrates foundational skills in user-centric problem-solving that are highly transferable to my professional work in UX/UI design.
